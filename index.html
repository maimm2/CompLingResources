Before becoming a computational linguistics student, my background was linguistics, literature, and education. The decision to pursue computational lingusitics was not easy given the new skills I had to build and theoretical knowledge I had to learn related to computer science, however, I liked was deeply interested and decided to transition half way through my PhD. During this transition, I have learned a few things along the way, and decided to create this page for any others who are interested in computational linguistics but are unsure of what the journey will carry. This page may be helpful to you if you 1) are a linguist and 2) are interested in computational linguistics as a research area but do not come from a CS background or 3) decide to develop some computational tools skillset for linguistics research. My advisor Dr. Schwartz. has suggested almost all of the resources before, and I am very grateful for his patience on this journey of exploration and learning.

General Considerations

  • Pick a kind and patient advisor. This is not just for computational linguistics, but it will matter more if you are learning and pursuing a computational lingusitics degree when you are starting without a CS background. You will need an advisor who is on board and is willing to work with you rather than tear you down.
  • Learn about the system. Computational linguistics follows a different system than other linguistic disciplines. Learn about them, and speak to other computational linguistics students as much as you can.
  • Understand yourself and how you learn best.
  • Do not get overwhelmed by the amount of Math.
